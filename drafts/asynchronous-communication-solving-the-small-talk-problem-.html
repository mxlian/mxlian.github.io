Asynchronous communication: solving the small talk problem

This 'new' (come on, it exists since ever) of communication is awesome. SMS,
Whatsapp, whatever... Doesn't require your immediate attention. You don't get interrupted.

But what I've recently came to realize is its efficiency in terms of conversation overhead.

It removes a lot of the useless social formalities, which I hate.

It's specially annoying when speaking with someone I haven't seen in a long time. More often than not the situation requires to feed the minutes of the conversation with nonsense small talk. I can't explain it entirely but avoiding it, creates a bad atmosphere. There is some time involved to let the trust emerge again, which is proportional to the amount of time passed from the last conversation and inversely proportional to the counterpart coolness.

Interestingly this small talk is not required when chatting.

I noticed it a lot when speaking with my family. Before we went to asynchronous channels I have found myself multiple times procrastinating the fact I should call them because I didn't wanted to go through this nonsense talk about the weather or what did they eat.

It doesn't replace completely old fashioned channels but it makes my live so much better most of the time.
